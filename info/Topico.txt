Topic 2: Climate Change and Extreme Weather Events

Research Question: How have the frequency and economic damage of extreme weather events changed over the past 50 years, and which world regions show the steepest acceleration?

Data Sources:
- EM-DAT International Disaster Database — emdat.be: Records of natural disasters worldwide since 1900, including death toll, affected population, and economic damage.
- NOAA Global Surface Temperature Dataset — ncei.noaa.gov: Monthly global and regional
temperature anomalies from 1880 to present.
- Our World in Data — Natural Disasters — ourworldindata.org/natural-disasters: Cleaned, ready-touse CSVs derived from EM-DAT.

Suggested Visualizations:
. Choropleth comparing disaster frequency across two 25-year periods (1970–1995 vs. 1996–2024).
. Area chart showing cumulative economic damage by disaster type over decades.
. Scatter plot: temperature anomaly vs. number of extreme events per year.
. Small multiples: trend lines for each disaster type (flood, storm, wildfire, drought).

Guide for Students: Your IEEE article must explicitly state in the Discussion that correlation between temperature rise and disaster frequency does not imply direct causation — address confounding variables (better reporting systems, population growth in exposed areas). Cite IPCC Assessment Reports as foundational Related Work. The choropleth before/after comparison is a compelling IEEE opening figure. Be precise: separate economic damage from human casualties, as they tell different stories.

Pitfalls and Ethics:
Reporting bias: disasters in wealthy countries are better documented than in poorer ones.
Do not conflate weather (short-term events) with climate (long-term patterns).
Avoid implying causation from correlation in scatter plots — always add a caveat.

Orientação para o projeto

Decisões gerais:
- Eventos extremos: Droughts, Floods, Storms, Extreme Temperatures, Wildfires (mais representativos e mais ligados às mudanças climáticas)

- Período temporal: 1975 a 2024 (50 anos). Comparar dois perídos de 25 anos (1975 a 1999 e 2000 a 2024)

- Área geográfica: Divisão em continentes - África, Ásia, Europa, América do Norte, América do Sul e Oceania.

- Gases do efeito estufa (greenhouse gases): CO2, Metano e Óxido Nitroso.

Objetivo: Mostrar se as alterações climáticas observadas no período temporal determinado seguem uma mesma tendência e se estão correlacionadas com o número de mortes e os prejuízos causados pelos eventos climáticos extremos
